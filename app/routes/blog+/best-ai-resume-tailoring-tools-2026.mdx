## What Actually Matters When Choosing

Before diving into individual tools, here's what actually differentiates them:

- **Speed:** Time from pasting a job posting to having a tailored resume ready. If you're applying to 50+ jobs, even 5 extra minutes per application adds up to hours.

- **ATS optimization:** Does it score your resume against the job description? More importantly, does it tell you *why* you're scoring low and what to fix?

- **Keyword matching intelligence:** Basic tools do literal string matching. Better tools understand that "project management" and "managed projects" are related.

- **Output quality:** AI can generate grammatically correct garbage. The question is whether the tailored bullets sound like something you'd actually say.

- **Free tier usefulness:** Can you actually tailor a resume and download it without paying? Or does "free" mean "free to look at a demo"?

- **Privacy:** Your resume contains your employment history and contact info. What happens to that data?

## Individual Tool Breakdowns

### Huntr

**Best for:** Job seekers who want resume tailoring integrated with comprehensive application tracking

**What it does well:**
- Kanban board for tracking applications — see your entire pipeline at a glance
- Chrome extension (4.8/5 stars, 1,100+ reviews) auto-captures job postings
- Dual ATS scoring: Base ATS Score for formatting + Job Match Score for alignment
- Semantic matching — recognizes "managed team" and "team management" as related

**Where it falls short:**
- Cover letter generation is "hit or miss"
- Mobile experience is limited
- At $40/month, it's among the more expensive options
- Some users report scoring "overstates alignment"

**Pricing:** Free: 2 AI-tailored resumes, 10 AI bullets | $40/mo, $90/quarter, $160/6 months

> **My take:** If you're running an organized, high-volume job search and want everything in one place, Huntr is the most complete solution. If you just need fast tailoring, it's overkill.

### Teal

**Best for:** Job seekers wanting a generous free tier with job tracking who don't mind manual tailoring

**What it does well:**
- Most generous free tier: unlimited resume downloads, unlimited job tracking, no credit card
- Side-by-side view showing your resume next to the job description
- Chrome extension (4.9/5 stars, 3,100+ users) works across 40+ job boards
- 0-100% Match Score with 15+ targeted checks

**Where it falls short:**
- AI content is frequently "generic and needs manual revision"
- One user reported templates "can't be read by common ATS systems like Workday"
- $29/month draws criticism as "too expensive"
- Multiple users report billing issues after cancellation attempts

**Pricing:** Free: unlimited downloads | $29/mo, $79/quarter, $13/week. 7-day free trial available.

> **My take:** Teal wins on free tier generosity. If you're willing to use AI suggestions as starting points and do your own editing, you can accomplish real work without paying.

### Rezi

**Best for:** Budget-conscious users planning multiple job searches who want to pay once and own forever

**What it does well:**
- $149 lifetime deal eliminates subscription fatigue
- Proprietary Rezi Score across 23 criteria points
- Keyword priority scoring (1-3 scale) distinguishes requirements from nice-to-haves
- Founder actively engages on Reddit — unusual transparency

**Where it falls short:**
- Limited templates compared to design-focused competitors
- AI content "often produces generic content requiring significant human refinement"
- Chrome extension rates just 1.9-2.1/5 stars
- Free tier is restrictive: only 1 resume with 3 PDF downloads

**Pricing:** Free: 1 resume, 3 PDFs, limited AI | $29/mo or $149 lifetime

> **My take:** If you job search every 2-3 years, the lifetime deal is the obvious choice. $149 amortized over 3 job searches is $50 per search — cheaper than any monthly subscription.

### Jobscan

**Where it falls short:**
- Most expensive at $49.95/month
- Free tier is extremely restrictive: 5 scans initially, then just 2 per month
- Keyword matching is literal — "the AI lacks context"
- Polarizing: 4.4/5 on Trustpilot but 2.7/5 on SiteJabber with billing complaints

**Pricing:** Free: 5 scans, then 2/month | $49.95/mo or $89.95/quarter

> **My take:** If you have a finished resume and want to know exactly why it's not passing ATS filters, Jobscan provides the most detailed analysis. Use it for final checks on high-priority applications.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What is the best free AI resume tailor?**
A: Teal offers the most usable free tier with unlimited downloads and job tracking included. Instaresume is truly free without upsell pressure but has fewer features. Resume Tailor lets you build and download unlimited basic resumes free — AI tailoring suggestions are limited on the free tier.

**Q: How does AI resume tailoring work?**
A: AI resume tailoring analyzes a job description to identify key requirements, skills, and keywords. It then compares your resume against those requirements and suggests edits — rewriting bullets to include missing keywords, reordering sections to emphasize relevant experience, or flagging gaps. Quality varies significantly between tools.

**Q: Is it worth paying for a resume tailoring subscription?**
A: If you're applying to 20+ jobs, paid features save significant time. At $15-30/month, even saving 30 minutes per application across 20 applications saves 10+ hours — worth more than the subscription cost. For occasional job searches, use free tiers or Rezi's lifetime deal.

**Q: Which resume tailor has the best ATS optimization?**
A: Jobscan has the most detailed ATS analysis — it's reverse-engineered major ATS systems and provides system-specific tips. Rezi's 23-criteria scoring is also comprehensive. Most other tools provide basic keyword matching.

**Q: Can AI-tailored resumes pass ATS systems?**
A: Yes, if done correctly. ATS systems primarily scan for keyword matches and parseable formatting. AI tailoring helps with keywords; you still need clean formatting. The risk is AI generating unnatural keyword stuffing that passes ATS but turns off human reviewers.

**Q: How long does it take to tailor a resume with AI?**
A: With most tools: 5-15 minutes from pasting a job description to downloading a tailored PDF. Faster tools (Resume Tailor, Instaresume) minimize clicks. More comprehensive tools (Huntr, Teal) add time navigating dashboards.

**Q: What's the difference between resume tailoring and resume building?**
A: Resume building creates a resume from scratch. Resume tailoring takes an existing resume and modifies it for a specific job — adjusting keywords, reordering bullets, emphasizing relevant experience. Most tools now do both.

**Q: Is Resume Tailor free?**
A: Yes, you can build and download unlimited resumes free — no credit card required. AI-powered tailoring features are limited on the free tier; full AI features require the paid plan ($4.99/week or $15/month).
